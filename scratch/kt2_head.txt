        <p style="margin: 0 0 4px 0; font-size: 7.2pt; line-height: 1.3; text-align: justify; color: #374151;">Tensions rose at the Cairo Conference in 1964, which established the <strong>PLO</strong> (Palestine Liberation Organization) to coordinate Palestinian resistance. Arab League states also agreed to divert the headwaters of the <strong>Jordan River</strong> to deny water to Israel's National Water Carrier, sparking frequent border skirmishes.</p>
      </div>
    
      <div style="margin-bottom: 8px;">
        <h4 style="margin: 0 0 3px 0; font-size: 7.8pt; color: #111827; font-weight: bold; border-bottom: 1px solid #e5e7eb; padding-bottom: 1px;">2. Border Skirmishes & samu reprisal (1966â€“1967)</h4>
        <p style="margin: 0 0 4px 0; font-size: 7.2pt; line-height: 1.3; text-align: justify; color: #374151;">Syria actively backed Arafat's Fatah group, allowing guerrilla raids into northern Israel. The IDF responded with harsh reprisals, including the <strong>Samu Raid</strong> in Jordan in November 1966. Tensions escalated on 7 April 1967 when Israeli jets shot down six Syrian <strong>MiG fighters</strong> over Damascus during border artillery duels.</p>
      </div>
    
      <div style="margin-bottom: 8px;">
        <h4 style="margin: 0 0 3px 0; font-size: 7.8pt; color: #111827; font-weight: bold; border-bottom: 1px solid #e5e7eb; padding-bottom: 1px;">3. The May Mobilization & Blockade (1967)</h4>
        <p style="margin: 0 0 4px 0; font-size: 7.2pt; line-height: 1.3; text-align: justify; color: #374151;">In May 1967, the USSR falsely warned Egypt that Israel was massing troops to invade Syria. Nasser expelled UN peacekeepers (UNEF) from Sinai, deployed 100,000 troops, and closed the <strong>Straits of Tiran</strong> to Israeli shipping, blockading the port of Eilat. Jordan signed a mutual defense pact with Egypt, creating an encirclement crisis.</p>
      </div>
    
      <div style="margin-bottom: 8px;">
        <h4 style="margin: 0 0 3px 0; font-size: 7.8pt; color: #111827; font-weight: bold; border-bottom: 1px solid #e5e7eb; padding-bottom: 1px;">4. Preemptive Strike & Six Days of Victory</h4>
        <p style="margin: 0 0 4px 0; font-size: 7.2pt; line-height: 1.3; text-align: justify; color: #374151;">On 5 June 1967, Israel launched a preemptive air attack, <strong>Operation Focus</strong>, destroying 90% of the Egyptian air force on the ground. Secure in the air, the IDF fought a three-front war. In six days, Israel captured the <strong>Sinai Peninsula</strong> and <strong>Gaza Strip</strong> from Egypt, the <strong>West Bank</strong> and East Jerusalem from Jordan, and the <strong>Golan Heights</strong> from Syria. This massive victory established Israeli military dominance and created 300,000 new refugees.</p>
