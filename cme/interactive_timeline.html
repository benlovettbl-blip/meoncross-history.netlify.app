1945–1947
The British Mandate Breaks Down
Click to reveal
  • Post-WWII Tension: Britain faces conflicting demands from Zionists (seeking a haven for Holocaust survivors) and Palestinian Arabs (demanding independence).
  • July 1946: The Jewish insurgent group Irgun bombs the British administrative headquarters at the King David Hotel in Jerusalem, killing 91 people and shattering British public morale.
  • Feb 1947: Exhausted by the insurgency and the cost of keeping 100,000 troops in Palestine, Britain hands the problem to the newly formed United Nations.
  • Nov 1947: The UN passes Resolution 181 (The Partition Plan), allocating 55% of the land to a Jewish state. Jews accept it; the Arab League rejects it completely, sparking immediate civil war.
1948–1949
The First Arab-Israeli War & The Nakba
Click to reveal
  • May 1948: The British Mandate officially ends. David Ben-Gurion declares the State of Israel. The next day, armies from five Arab nations invade.
  • 1948-49 War: Israel survives due to a unified command (creation of the Israeli Defence Forces - IDF), mandatory conscription, and importing Czech weapons during UN truces.
  • The Aftermath (1949): Armistice agreements establish the 'Green Line'. Israel expands its territory beyond the UN plan. Jordan annexes the West Bank; Egypt takes military control of Gaza.
  • The Nakba: Over 700,000 Palestinian Arabs flee or are driven from their homes, creating a massive refugee crisis. The UN sets up UNRWA to run refugee camps.
1950–1955
State Building, Border Wars & The Cold War
Click to reveal
  • 1950: Israel passes the Law of Return, granting any Jew worldwide the right to Israeli citizenship, leading to a massive demographic expansion.
  • 1954: Gamal Abdel Nasser officially takes power in Egypt, promoting Pan-Arabism to unite the Arab world.
  • Feb 1955: Israel launches a massive reprisal attack against Egyptian military headquarters in Gaza to stop cross-border 'Fedayeen' guerrilla raids.
  • Sept 1955: Humiliated by the Gaza raid, Nasser signs the massive Czech Arms Deal to bypass Western embargoes and secure advanced Soviet weapons, escalating Cold War tensions.
1956–1958
The Suez Crisis & Arab Nationalism
Click to reveal
  • July 1956: The USA and Britain withdraw funding for the Aswan High Dam. In retaliation, Nasser nationalises the Suez Canal to use its toll revenues.
  • Oct 1956: Britain, France, and Israel hold secret meetings and sign the Protocol of Sèvres to collude against Egypt.
  • Oct/Nov 1956 (Suez Crisis): Israel invades the Sinai Peninsula, destroying Fedayeen bases. Britain and France intervene but are forced to withdraw humiliatingly after immense pressure from the USA and USSR.
  • The Result: UN peacekeepers (UNEF) are stationed in the Sinai, giving Israel a decade of secure borders and reopening the Straits of Tiran for trade.
1964–1967 (May)
The Road to the Six Day War
Click to reveal
  • 1964: The Cairo Conference officially sets up the Palestine Liberation Organisation (PLO) to unite Palestinians.
  • 1965-66: Fatah begins sabotage raids. Disputes erupt over Syria's 'Headwater Diversion Plan'.
  • Nov 1966: The IDF launches a destructive reprisal raid on the West Bank village of Samu, humiliating King Hussein of Jordan.
  • May 1967 (The Crisis): Soviet misinformation falsely claims Israel is massing troops on the Syrian border. Nasser reacts by moving 100,000 troops into Sinai, ordering UNEF peacekeepers out, and blockading the Straits of Tiran.
1967 (June) – 1969
The Six Day War & The Aftermath
Click to reveal
  • 55 June 1967: Viewing the blockade as an act of war, Israel launches a devastating pre-emptive air strike, destroying Arab air forces on the ground in hours.
  • The Victory: In just six days, Israel captures the Sinai Peninsula, Gaza Strip, West Bank, East Jerusalem, and the Golan Heights.
  • Aug 1967: The Arab League meets in Khartoum and issues the 'Three Nos': no peace, no recognition, no negotiation with Israel.
  • Nov 1967: The UN passes Resolution 242, establishing the principle of 'Land for Peace'.
1970–1972
Global Terrorism & Black September
Click to reveal
  • Sept 1970: The PFLP hijacks international passenger jets to Dawson's Field in Jordan to gain global publicity.
  • Sept 1970 (Black September): Furious at the hijackings, King Hussein's army attacks the PLO in Jordan. The PLO is expelled and moves its headquarters to Lebanon.
  • Sept 1972: 'Black September' terrorists take Israeli athletes hostage at the Munich Olympics. 11 Israelis are killed.
1973–1975
The Yom Kippur War & Shuttle Diplomacy
Click to reveal
  • 6 Oct 1973: On the holiest Jewish holiday, Egypt and Syria launch a surprise coordinated attack, overwhelming Israeli defences initially.
  • Superpower Intervention: The USA launches a $2.2 billion emergency airlift of weapons to save Israel. The USSR arms the Arabs.
  • The Oil Crisis: In retaliation for US support of Israel, Arab OPEC nations launch a strict oil embargo, quadrupling global fuel prices.
  • 1974-75: US Secretary of State Henry Kissinger engages in 'Shuttle Diplomacy', leading to disengagement treaties and the reopening of the Suez Canal.
1977–1979
The Camp David Accords
Click to reveal
  • Nov 1977: Egyptian President Anwar Sadat makes a historic visit to Jerusalem, addressing the Israeli Knesset and breaking the psychological barrier.
  • Sept 1978: US President Jimmy Carter invites Sadat and Israeli PM Menachem Begin to Camp David to hammer out an agreement.
  • March 1979: The Treaty of Washington is signed. Egypt becomes the first Arab state to officially recognise Israel, and Israel withdraws from the Sinai Peninsula completely.
1978–1982
The PLO in Lebanon & Full-Scale Invasion
Click to reveal
  • 1970s Lebanon: The PLO establishes 'Fatahland' in southern Lebanon, launching Katyusha rockets into northern Israel.
  • March 1978: After the Coastal Road Massacre, Israel launches Operation Litani, a limited invasion to push the PLO back. UNIFIL is established.
  • June 1982: Following an assassination attempt on the Israeli ambassador in London, Israel launches a full-scale invasion: Operation Peace for Galilee.
  • Sept 1982: The IDF besieges Beirut, forcing the PLO to evacuate to Tunisia. Lebanese Christian militias carry out the horrific Sabra and Shatila massacres while the IDF guards the perimeters.
1987–1991
The Intifada & The End of the Cold War
Click to reveal
  • Dec 1987: An Israeli army truck crashes in Gaza, sparking the First Intifada ('shaking off'). This grassroots Palestinian uprising features mass strikes and stone-throwing.
  • 1988: Israel's harsh 'Iron Fist' military response draws intense international condemnation.
  • Dec 1988: Yasser Arafat addresses the UN, historically renouncing terrorism and accepting the two-state solution.
  • 1991: The collapse of the USSR (End of the Cold War) strips the PLO of funding. The US-led Gulf War victory allows America to pressure both sides to attend the Madrid Peace Conference.
1993–1995
The Oslo Accords & The Peace Process
Click to reveal
  • 1993: Frustrated by public stalls, Israeli and PLO negotiators hold secret, back-channel talks in Norway.
  • Sept 1993 (Oslo I): The Oslo Accords are signed. Arafat and Rabin shake hands on the White House lawn. The Palestinian National Authority (PNA) is set up.
  • Oct 1994: King Hussein signs a full peace treaty between Jordan and Israel.
  • Nov 1995: The peace process suffers a massive blow when Israeli PM Yitzhak Rabin is assassinated by Yigal Amir, a Jewish religious extremist.
