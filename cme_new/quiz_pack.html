Topic 1.1: Quiz Answer Key & Explanations

Q1: What legal authority was Britain given by the League of Nations in 1920 to govern Palestine?
Correct Answer: The British Mandate
The League of Nations created this system to manage former Ottoman territories, though in Palestine, it became an impossible balancing act between protecting the rights of the Arab majority and supporting Jewish immigration.
Q2: What was the name of the nationalist movement aiming to create a Jewish homeland in Palestine?
Correct Answer: Zionism
Originally a political movement founded in Europe in response to widespread anti-Semitism, it argued for the creation of a Jewish homeland in Palestine to ensure Jewish survival.
Q3: Which 1917 British declaration originally promised to support the establishment of a Jewish national home?
Correct Answer: The Balfour Declaration
The 1917 Balfour Declaration was highly controversial because it promised a Jewish national home in a land where the vast majority of the population was Arab, laying the groundwork for decades of future conflict.
Q4: The horrors of which event during the Second World War vastly increased global sympathy for a Jewish state?
Correct Answer: The Holocaust
The murder of six million European Jews generated immense international sympathy and moral pressure on Western nations, particularly the USA, to support a Jewish state as a safe haven.
Q5: In February 1947, exhausted by the insurgency, Britain handed the Palestine problem to which international organisation?
Correct Answer: The United Nations (UN)
The newly formed United Nations was handed the Palestine problem in 1947 because the British had exhausted their military troops and financial resources trying to suppress the Jewish insurgency.
Q6: What was the name of the moderate Jewish defence force that helped smuggle immigrants into Palestine?
Correct Answer: The Haganah
Formed in the 1920s originally to defend Jewish settlements, it evolved into a highly organised military force that helped smuggle Holocaust survivors past the British blockade.
Q7: Which extremist Jewish terrorist organisation bombed the British administrative headquarters in 1946?
Correct Answer: The Irgun
This right-wing paramilitary group believed that diplomacy had failed and that only armed force, sabotage, and acts of terror would force the British to abandon their control of Palestine.
Q8: In which city was this bombed headquarters, the King David Hotel, located?
Correct Answer: Jerusalem
A city of profound religious significance, making its political status one of the most explosive issues, which is why the UN later proposed it be an international zone.
Q9: What was the name of the ship carrying 4,500 Jewish refugees that the British notoriously turned back in 1947?
Correct Answer: SS Exodus
The highly publicised image of Holocaust survivors being forced back to Europe by the British Royal Navy was a massive public relations disaster for Britain and significantly boosted global support for Zionism.
Q10: What was the name of the 1937 British Commission that was the very first to recommend the partition of Palestine?
Correct Answer: The Peel Commission
The 1937 Peel Commission concluded that the mandate was fundamentally unworkable because the nationalist aspirations of the Arabs and Jews were entirely incompatible.
Q11: Exactly how many people died in the King David Hotel bombing?
Correct Answer: 91 people
The victims included Arabs, Britons, and Jews; the sheer scale of the death toll and the resulting negative publicity permanently destroyed British public morale to maintain the Mandate.
Q12: What was the name of the Austrian Jewish journalist who published The Jewish State in 1896, sparking the political Zionist movement?
Correct Answer: Theodor Herzl
As the visionary father of modern political Zionism, his writings successfully shifted the Jewish focus from a purely religious connection to the Holy Land toward a highly organised, secular political campaign.
Q13: Who was the British Foreign Secretary immediately after the Second World War who heavily clashed with Zionists?
Correct Answer: Ernest Bevin
Ernest Bevin was determined to maintain good relations with the Arab world to secure British access to vital Middle Eastern oil, causing bitter conflict over refugee quotas.
Q14: Exactly what percentage of Palestinian land was allocated to the proposed Jewish state under the UN Resolution 181 partition plan?
Correct Answer: 55 percent
Although Jews formed only about a third of the population, they were allocated the majority of the territory to provide enough space to absorb the anticipated influx of Holocaust survivors.
Q15: What is the specific Hebrew term for the Jewish agricultural communal farms that often acted as fortified defensive outposts?
Correct Answer: Kibbutz
These highly socialist, collective farming communities were crucial not only for agricultural development but also acted as strategic military outposts that delayed Arab advances in 1948.

Topic 1.2: Quiz Answer Key & Explanations

Q1: Which Arab country annexed the West Bank and East Jerusalem following the 1948-49 war?
Correct Answer: Jordan
King Abdullah formally annexed the territory in 1950 and granted Jordanian citizenship to its Palestinian residents, a move that angered other Arab nations.
Q2: Which Arab country took military control over the Gaza Strip?
Correct Answer: Egypt
Unlike Jordan, Egypt did not annex the territory but retained it under a strict military administration, leaving the Palestinians there as stateless refugees.
Q3: What colour was used to describe the 1949 armistice lines that formed Israel's new borders?
Correct Answer: The Green Line
These armistice lines constituted Israel's de facto borders with its Arab neighbours from 1949 until they were shattered during the June War of 1967.
Q4: What Arabic phrase, meaning 'the catastrophe', do Palestinians use to describe their mass displacement in the 1948 war?
Correct Answer: Al-Nakba
This term encapsulates not just the military defeat, but the permanent loss of the Palestinian homeland and the fragmentation of their society.
Q5: What 1950 Law gave any Jew worldwide the right to Israeli citizenship?
Correct Answer: The Law of Return
The Law of Return was the ultimate fulfilment of the Zionist dream, legally enshrining Israel as the homeland for the global Jewish diaspora and ensuring a rapid demographic expansion.
Q6: Exactly how many Palestinian Arabs fled or were driven from their homes?
Correct Answer: Over 700,000
Approximately 700,000 to 750,000 Palestinians became refugees, representing the vast majority of the Arab population that had lived in the territory that became Israel.
Q7: What is the acronym of the UN agency created in December 1949 to provide camps and relief for these refugees?
Correct Answer: UNRWA
The United Nations Relief and Works Agency became a permanent fixture providing food, shelter, and education as the conflict dragged on.
Q8: What is the name of the new unified national army created by David Ben-Gurion during the war?
Correct Answer: Israeli Defence Forces (IDF)
The Israeli Defence Forces were formed in May 1948 when Ben-Gurion unified the Haganah and smaller militant groups, ensuring the government had total control over a single military.
Q9: To build up its military strength, what mandatory service did Israel introduce for all 18-year-old men and women?
Correct Answer: Compulsory conscription
By requiring men and women to serve and maintaining a lifelong reserve duty, Israel turned almost its entire adult population into a rapid-response military force.
Q10: What is the Arabic name for the Palestinian guerrilla fighters who launched raids into Israel from Egyptian-controlled Gaza?
Correct Answer: Fedayeen
Translating to 'those who sacrifice themselves', these fighters were viewed as terrorists by Israelis, but celebrated as resistance heroes across the Arab world.
Q11: What was the specific military codename for the severe Israeli reprisal attack on the Egyptian army headquarters in Gaza on 28 February 1955?
Correct Answer: Operation Black Arrow
Operation Black Arrow was a massive turning point in Egyptian-Israeli relations, convincing Nasser that Egypt's military was too weak and prompting the Czech arms deal.
Q12: Who was the Israeli commander of the Paratroopers Brigade that led this 1955 attack on Gaza, and who later became a highly controversial political figure?
Correct Answer: Ariel Sharon
Ariel Sharon's aggressive leadership established his reputation as a ruthless tactician, foreshadowing his later role in the 1982 invasion of Lebanon.
Q13: What was the name of the Israeli southern port that was economically strangled by Egypt's blockade of the Straits of Tiran?
Correct Answer: Eilat
Because it was Israel's only gateway to the Red Sea, the economic strangulation of Eilat cut Israel off from vital trade routes to Asia and East Africa.
Q14: Who was the Egyptian King overthrown in 1952, partly because of the army's anger over his failures in the 1948 war?
Correct Answer: King Farouk
The humiliating defeat deeply shamed the Egyptian military, prompting a group of 'Free Officers' to launch a revolution that paved the way for Nasser's rise to power.
Q15: What was the name of the 1952 financial agreement in which West Germany agreed to pay compensation to Israel for the Holocaust?
Correct Answer: The Reparations Agreement
The Reparations Agreement provided Israel with hundreds of millions of dollars in vital foreign currency and equipment, essential for absorbing massive immigration.

Topic 1.3: Quiz Answer Key & Explanations

Q1: Who is the Egyptian leader who became President in 1954 and championed Arab nationalism?
Correct Answer: Gamal Abdel Nasser
Gamal Abdel Nasser's charismatic leadership transformed Egypt into the dominant power in the Middle East, challenging both Israeli security and Western imperialism.
Q2: What specific political ideology did this leader promote to unite Arabic-speaking nations against Western influence and Israel?
Correct Answer: Pan-Arabism
This movement sought to politically and economically unify the Arab world, presenting a severe strategic threat to Israel by attempting to surround it.
Q3: What major infrastructure project did Egypt plan to build on the River Nile to control flooding and provide hydroelectric power?
Correct Answer: The Aswan High Dam
The Aswan High Dam was Nasser's flagship domestic project, intended to modernize the Egyptian economy and alleviate poverty.
Q4: In retaliation for this withdrawn funding, what vital waterway did the Egyptian President nationalize in July 1956?
Correct Answer: The Suez Canal
By nationalising the canal to use its toll revenues, Nasser struck a massive blow against British and French imperial pride and their oil supply routes.
Q5: On 29 October 1956, Israel invaded which Egyptian territory as the first stage of this secret military plan?
Correct Answer: The Sinai Peninsula
The IDF's rapid and stunning conquest of the Sinai Peninsula proved their military superiority, allowing them to destroy Fedayeen guerrilla bases.
Q6: In February 1955, the Israeli army launched a massive retaliation raid against the Egyptian military headquarters in which territory?
Correct Answer: Gaza
On 28 February 1955, the severe IDF surprise attack on an Egyptian base in Gaza killed 38 Egyptian soldiers, a deep humiliation that exposed military weakness and convinced Nasser to sign the Czech Arms Deal.
Q7: From which Eastern Bloc country did Egypt sign a massive arms deal in September 1955 to bypass Western arms embargoes?
Correct Answer: Czechoslovakia
Acting as a proxy for the Soviet Union, Czechoslovakia provided Egypt with advanced jet fighters and tanks, fundamentally shifting the balance of military power.
Q8: Which global superpower famously withdrew its offer to fund this dam in July 1956 due to Egypt's growing ties with the Communist bloc?
Correct Answer: The USA
President Eisenhower withdrew the loan offer to punish Nasser for buying Soviet arms, demonstrating how Middle Eastern nations were used in Cold War rivalries.
Q9: What acronym represents the United Nations peacekeeping force stationed on the Egyptian-Israeli border after the crisis?
Correct Answer: UNEF (United Nations Emergency Force)
The United Nations Emergency Force successfully acted as a buffer between Israel and Egypt, providing Israel with a decade of secure southern borders.
Q10: What is the specific name of the Egyptian town located at the extreme southern tip of the Sinai Peninsula, captured by Israel in 1956 to break the naval blockade?
Correct Answer: Sharm el-Sheikh
Controlling this heavily fortified outpost was the only way to dominate the narrow Straits of Tiran, making its capture the ultimate strategic prize.
Q11: What is the name of the secret agreement signed in October 1956 between Britain, France, and Israel to collude against Egypt?
Correct Answer: Protocol of Sèvres
The Protocol of Sèvres planned for Israel to invade Egypt, providing Britain and France with a manufactured 'excuse' to intervene militarily to seize the canal.
Q12: What was the specific operational codename for the devastating Israeli attack on Syrian outposts near the Sea of Galilee in December 1955?
Correct Answer: Operation Kinneret (Olive Leaves)
Operation Kinneret was intended to stop Syrian troops firing on Israeli fishermen, but the ferocity of the attack pushed Syria closer to Egypt.
Q13: Who was the moderate Israeli Prime Minister who preferred diplomacy but was overruled by hardliners in 1954-55?
Correct Answer: Moshe Sharett
Moshe Sharett's tenure was marked by a constant struggle against the aggressive, retaliatory defence policies championed by Moshe Dayan and David Ben-Gurion.
Q14: What was the official Israeli military codename for their invasion of the Sinai Peninsula in October 1956?
Correct Answer: Operation Kadesh
Named after the biblical oasis, Operation Kadesh relied heavily on rapid armored thrusts and paratroop drops to overwhelm Egyptian forces.
Q15: Under the secret Protocol of Sèvres, what specific diplomatic mechanism were Britain and France going to use as a false pretext to invade?
Correct Answer: An Anglo-French ultimatum
Britain and France issued a pre-planned 'ultimatum' demanding both sides withdraw from the canal, knowing Egypt would refuse on its own territory.

Topic 2.1: Quiz Answer Key & Explanations

Q1: What umbrella organisation was set up in 1964 to unite the Palestinian people?
Correct Answer: The PLO (Palestine Liberation Organisation)
The Palestine Liberation Organisation quickly became an umbrella for various independent guerrilla groups dedicated to armed struggle.
Q2: What is the name of the Palestinian guerrilla group, founded by Yasser Arafat, that began raiding Israel in 1965?
Correct Answer: Fatah
Believing in using guerrilla warfare and sabotage against Israeli infrastructure, Fatah launched over 100 raids between 1965 and 1967.
Q3: Which narrow waterway did Egypt blockade on 22 May 1967, cutting off Israel's vital trade route to the Red Sea?
Correct Answer: The Straits of Tiran
Nesser's closure of the Straits of Tiran cut off Israel's oil supply; Israel had explicitly warned that blockading this waterway would be treated as an act of war.
Q4: What tactical advantage was crucial to the success of the initial Israeli attack on 5 June 1967?
Correct Answer: Total surprise
By attacking at 7:45 AM while Egyptian pilots were eating breakfast, Israel achieved total tactical surprise, destroying the Arab air forces within hours.
Q5: What type of pre-emptive attack did Israel use to destroy the Egyptian air force on the ground?
Correct Answer: A pre-emptive airstrike
This devastating pre-emptive strike gave the IDF total air superiority, leaving Arab ground troops completely defenseless.
Q6: What was the location of the 1964 Arab League Conference that officially set an anti-Israel agenda?
Correct Answer: Cairo
The 1964 conference marked a turning point where Arab leaders officially sponsored Palestinian nationalism and agreed on a unified military strategy.
Q7: The diversion of which vital river by Syria and Lebanon caused major border tensions with Israel?
Correct Answer: The River Jordan
The Headwater Diversion Plan threatened to cut off Israel's fresh water supply, prompting Israel to use airstrikes to destroy the engineering works.
Q8: What was the name of the West Bank village where the IDF launched a massive, destructive reprisal raid in November 1966?
Correct Answer: Samu
On 13 November 1966, the IDF launched a massive raid on the village of Samu in retaliation for a Fatah landmine, clashing with the Jordanian army, destroying dozens of houses, and killing 15 Jordanian soldiers. This humiliated King Hussein and drew international condemnation.
Q9: What type of Soviet-made fighter jets did Israel shoot down six of over Damascus on 7 April 1967?
Correct Answer: Syrian MiGs
Humiliated by the loss of six MiG-21 fighter jets on 7 April 1967 and the Israeli planes flying low over Damascus, Syria demanded that Egypt honor their mutual defense pact.
Q10: In May 1967, which global superpower deliberately gave Egypt false intelligence claiming Israel was massing troops?
Correct Answer: The USSR (Soviet Union)
On 13 May 1967, the Soviet Union falsely warned Egypt that Israel was massing troops on the Syrian border, which prompted Nasser to mobilize his forces in the Sinai on 15 May.
Q11: Who was the Israeli Prime Minister who initially hesitated to go to war in May 1967, facing immense domestic criticism?
Correct Answer: Levi Eshkol
Levi Eshkol's cautious approach frustrated Israeli military leaders, forcing him to yield the Defence Ministry portfolio to Moshe Dayan.
Q12: What specific Arab military alliance, formed between Egypt and Syria in November 1966, meant an attack on one would bring the other into war?
Correct Answer: The Egypt-Syria Defence Pact
The Egypt-Syria Defence Pact was a crucial tripwire, meaning border skirmishes with Syria threatened to drag the entire Egyptian army into conflict.
Q13: What was the specific military codename for the highly secretive, pre-emptive Israeli airstrike on 5 June 1967?
Correct Answer: Operation Focus
Operation Focus relied on total radio silence and flying under Egyptian radar, resulting in the destruction of over 300 planes.
Q14: How many Egyptian troops did Nasser move into the Sinai Peninsula by mid-May 1967 after receiving the false intelligence?
Correct Answer: 100,000 troops
The sudden deployment of 100,000 Egyptian soldiers removed Israel's strategic depth, causing sheer panic and forcing a massive mobilisation.
Q15: Who was the American President who suggested Israel wait for an international flotilla, a plan that failed?
Correct Answer: Lyndon B. Johnson
President Lyndon B. Johnson was bogged down in Vietnam and could only offer vague promises, leaving Israel to act alone.

Topic 2.2: Quiz Answer Key & Explanations

Q1: What was the number of the UN Resolution passed in November 1967 that established the principle of 'land for peace'?
Correct Answer: UN Resolution 242
Although the cornerstone of future peace negotiations, its ambiguous wording allowed Israel to justify retaining captured land until treaties were signed.
Q2: Which occupied territory provided Israel with a massive physical military buffer zone against Egypt and contained valuable oil reserves?
Correct Answer: The Sinai Peninsula
Occupying the Sinai gave Israel immense strategic depth, meaning an Egyptian invasion would have to cross hundreds of miles of desert.
Q3: Which elevated occupied territory provided crucial fresh water sources and stopped Syrian artillery attacks on Israeli farming communities?
Correct Answer: The Golan Heights
Controlling the Golan Heights completely shifted the tactical advantage, placing Israeli forces less than 40 miles from Damascus.
Q4: The 1967 war created over 300,000 new Palestinian what, the vast majority of whom fled into neighbouring Jordan?
Correct Answer: Refugees
This second massive wave of displacement compounded the tragedy of the Nakba, overflowing UNRWA camps and creating recruiting grounds for militants.
Q5: In which West German city did the 'Black September' group strike during the highly publicised 1972 Olympic Games?
Correct Answer: Munich
The horrific reality of the hostage crisis playing out on live global television shocked the world and cast the PLO as a terrorist organisation.
Q6: In which city did the Arab League meet in August 1967, issuing the famous 'Three Nos' (no peace, no recognition, no negotiation)?
Correct Answer: Khartoum
This defiant resolution convinced many Israelis that the Arab world was completely unwilling to compromise.
Q7: What name is given to the prolonged artillery and aerial conflict (1967-1970) initiated by Egypt to wear down Israeli forces?
Correct Answer: The War of Attrition
President Nasser hoped this static war of constant shelling would break Israeli public morale and force a withdrawal from Sinai.
Q8: Realising conventional armies could not defeat Israel, Palestinian nationalist groups increasingly shifted to what violent tactic?
Correct Answer: International terrorism
Groups like Fatah and the PFLP adopted asymmetrical guerrilla warfare and international terror attacks to force the world to address their statelessness.
Q9: Which radical Marxist Palestinian organisation, founded by George Habash, pioneered the hijacking of international civilian flights?
Correct Answer: The PFLP
The Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine viewed attacking Western commercial airlines as a legitimate way to strike at Israel's supporters.
Q10: At which Jordanian desert airfield did this group blow up three hijacked international passenger airliners in September 1970?
Correct Answer: Dawson's Field
The spectacular visual gained massive media attention but humiliated the Jordanian government by proving they lost control of their territory.
Q11: Who was the Swedish UN diplomat appointed in November 1967 to try and implement Resolution 242?
Correct Answer: Gunnar Jarring
Gunnar Jarring spent over three years attempting to find a compromise, but his mission collapsed due to the rigid stances of both sides.
Q12: Who was the founder of the PFLP, a Palestinian Christian doctor who believed only Marxist revolution would liberate Palestine?
Correct Answer: George Habash
George Habash formed the PFLP in 1967, breaking away from Arafat's Fatah because he believed the PLO was too moderate.
