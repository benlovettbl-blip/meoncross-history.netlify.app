2.1 The Print Revolution

Chronological Big Picture

In the mid-15th century, German goldsmith Johannes Gutenberg developed the movable type printing press in Mainz. This invention sparked a massive transformation in Europe by making the replication of texts fast and affordable:

- Before the press, books were copied by hand, meaning they were rare, expensive, and mostly owned by the Church.

- The printing press allowed ideas (scientific, religious, and political) to spread quickly across national borders.

- It directly supported the spread of Protestant ideas during the Reformation.
