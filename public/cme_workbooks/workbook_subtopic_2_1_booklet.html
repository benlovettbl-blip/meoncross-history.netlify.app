Section 1: Historical Narrative & Core Knowledge
1. PLO Formation & Water Conflict (1964)

Tensions rose at the Cairo Conference in 1964, which established the PLO (Palestine Liberation Organization) to coordinate Palestinian resistance. Arab League states also agreed to divert the headwaters of the Jordan River to deny water to Israel's National Water Carrier, sparking frequent border skirmishes.

2. Border Skirmishes & samu reprisal (1966–1967)

Syria actively backed Arafat's Fatah group, allowing guerrilla raids into northern Israel. The IDF responded with harsh reprisals, including the Samu Raid in Jordan in November 1966. Tensions escalated on 7 April 1967 when Israeli jets shot down six Syrian MiG fighters over Damascus during border artillery duels.

3. The May Mobilization & Blockade (1967)

In May 1967, the USSR falsely warned Egypt that Israel was massing troops to invade Syria. Nasser expelled UN peacekeepers (UNEF) from Sinai, deployed 100,000 troops, and closed the Straits of Tiran to Israeli shipping, blockading the port of Eilat. Jordan signed a mutual defense pact with Egypt, creating an encirclement crisis.

4. Preemptive Strike & Six Days of Victory

On 5 June 1967, Israel launched a preemptive air attack, Operation Focus, destroying 90% of the Egyptian air force on the ground. Secure in the air, the IDF fought a three-front war. In six days, Israel captured the Sinai Peninsula and Gaza Strip from Egypt, the West Bank and East Jerusalem from Jordan, and the Golan Heights from Syria. This massive victory established Israeli military dominance and created 300,000 new refugees.

Vocabulary Focus

Cairo Conference: 1964 Arab League summit that founded the PLO and initiated water diversion plans.
PLO: Palestine Liberation Organization, founded to coordinate national resistance.
Jordan River: Waterway whose headwaters were contested in the border water wars.
Samu Raid: Devastating 1966 Israeli reprisal raid in the Jordanian-controlled West Bank.
MiG fighters: Soviet jet fighters flown by Syria, six of which were shot down in April 1967.
Straits of Tiran: Egyptian waterway closed by Nasser in May 1967, blockading Eilat port.
Operation Focus: Israel's preemptive airstrike on 5 June 1967 that destroyed Arab air capabilities.
Sinai Peninsula: Vast Egyptian desert territory captured by Israel.
Gaza Strip: Densely populated coastal strip captured from Egypt.
West Bank: Territory captured from Jordan, placing 1 million Palestinians under military rule.
Golan Heights: Strategic high ground captured from Syria in northern Israel.
Preemptive Strike: A military strike launched to prevent an expected imminent attack by an enemy.

Source A: PLO Charter (1964)

Source B: Nasser Speech (22 May 1967)

""We have expelled the UN forces. The Straits of Tiran are closed. Under no circumstances will we allow the Israeli flag to pass through the Gulf of Aqaba. If Israel wants war, we are ready.""

Source C: Levi Eshkol Statement (June 1967)

""The blockade of the Straits is a clear act of aggression. Surrounded by armies mobilized for our destruction, we had no choice but to strike first to secure our survival.""

Source D: UN Resolution 242 (Nov 1967)

""The fulfillment of Charter principles requires the establishment of a just peace which should include: (i) Withdrawal of Israel armed forces from territories occupied in the recent conflict; (ii) Termination of all claims of belligerency.""
