## 4.1 Segregation and Civil Rights

In the mid-20th century, Black Americans faced institutionalized segregation and disenfranchisement, particularly in the Southern United States. This was enforced by local Jim Crow laws:

- Public facilities, schools, and transport were segregated.
- Black citizens were systematically prevented from voting through literacy tests, poll taxes, and intimidation.
- Activists organized non-violent direct actions, such as the Montgomery Bus Boycott (1955-1956) and the Greensboro sit-ins (1960), to challenge these laws.

### Vocabulary
- **Segregation**: The legal or social separation of people based on race.
- **Disenfranchisement**: Depriving someone of the right to vote.
