## 1.1 The Claimants to the Throne

In January 1066, King Edward the Confessor died without leaving a direct heir. This sparked a succession crisis as three main claimants competed for the English throne:

1. **Harold Godwinson**, the Earl of Wessex: Crowned immediately as King Harold II with the support of the English council (the Witan).
2. **William**, Duke of Normandy: Claimed Edward had promised him the throne in 1051, and that Harold Godwinson had sworn a sacred oath to support his claim.
3. **Harald Hardrada**, the King of Norway: A Viking warrior who claimed the throne based on an old agreement between his predecessor and the former Danish rulers of England.

### Fill-in-the-Blanks
Text: King Edward the **[ 1 ]** died in January 1066. The English nobles chose **[ 2 ]** Godwinson as the new king, but William of **[ 3 ]** claimed the throne was rightfully his.
Words: Confessor, Harold, Normandy

### Vocabulary
- **Succession**: The order or process of inheriting a title, office, or throne.
- **Claimant**: A person who asserts a right to a title or throne.
- **Witan**: The council of English nobles and bishops who advised the Anglo-Saxon kings.

### True or False
- Edward the Confessor had a son who immediately inherited the crown. (False)
- Harald Hardrada was a claimant from Norway. (True)

## 1.2 The Battle of Hastings

William of Normandy prepared an invasion fleet. At the same time, Harald Hardrada invaded the north of England, prompting Harold Godwinson to march north and defeat the Vikings at the Battle of Stamford Bridge.

While Harold was in the north, William landed his forces on the south coast at Pevensey. Harold rushed south, and the two armies met at Senlac Hill near Hastings on 14 October 1066.

The Anglo-Saxons formed a strong shield wall at the top of the hill. The Normans failed to break it until they used the tactic of the **feigned retreat**—pretending to run away to lure the Saxons down the hill. Once the shield wall broke, King Harold was killed, and the Normans emerged victorious.

### Fill-in-the-Blanks
Text: The Anglo-Saxons established a strong **[ 1 ]** wall at the top of the hill, but were lured down when the Normans performed a **[ 2 ]** retreat. King **[ 3 ]** was killed in the battle.
Words: Shield, Feigned, Harold

### Vocabulary
- **Shield Wall**: A defensive tactic where soldiers overlap shields to form a solid barrier.
- **Feigned Retreat**: A tactical withdrawal where soldiers pretend to flee to draw out defenders.

### True or False
- The Battle of Hastings took place in October 1066. (True)
- The Normans easily broke the Saxon shield wall in their very first charge. (False)

### Retrieval Questions

  - **Answer**: Feigned retreat
  - **Distractor**: Shield wall

  - **Explanation**: The feigned retreat lured the Saxons down Senlac Hill, breaking their defensive line.

### Timeline
- **1066-09**: Battle of Stamford Bridge (defeat of Harald Hardrada).
- **1066-10**: Battle of Hastings (death of Harold II).
